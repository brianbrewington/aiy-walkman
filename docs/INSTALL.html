Set up your Walkman 🎵

A little music box with one button. Follow these steps once to teach it your YouTube Music, and it'll play your playlist every time you turn it on. No experience needed — read each part, including the little "Why?" notes that explain what's going on.

What is this thing?

It's a tiny computer (a Raspberry Pi) wired to a speaker/headphone board and one big button with a glowing ring. When it's set up, you power it on and music just plays. The button's light tells you what's happening, and the button itself is the only control.

Right now the light is blinking magenta. That's the box saying: "I don't have a YouTube Music login yet — set me up!" That's what this guide does.

What you'll need

  • The Walkman box, plugged in and on your home Wi-Fi (it joins automatically).
  • A Mac (these steps use the Mac's Terminal; Windows works too but the commands differ).
  • A YouTube Music account you can log into in a web browser.
  • About 10 minutes.

A few words first (so the steps make sense)

Why — what's a "cookie"?
When you log into a website, your browser saves a small secret file called a cookie. Every time you return, the browser shows the cookie so the site knows it's still you — that's why you don't have to log in every single time. Our box can't open a browser, so we'll copy your YouTube Music cookie onto the box so it can load your music as you. Treat that cookie like a house key: anyone who has it can use your account, so don't post it anywhere. (It also expires after a while — when it does, the light turns magenta again and you just redo the cookie steps.)
Why — what's a "playlist ID"?
Every YouTube Music playlist has a unique code in its web address. The box needs that code to know which playlist to play. You'll grab it in Step 1.
Why — what's "Terminal / SSH"?
Terminal is a Mac app where you type commands instead of clicking. SSH is a way to safely send commands to another computer over your network — here, the box. We'll use it to hand the box your cookie and playlist.
1

Get your playlist ID

  1. In a browser, open music.youtube.com and sign in.
  2. Open the playlist you want the box to play.
  3. Look at the address bar. The URL looks like:
    https://music.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLxxxxxxxxxxxx
  4. The part after list= (starting with PL, VL, or similar) is your playlist ID. Copy it somewhere — you'll paste it in Step 3.
2

Export your cookie

  1. In Chrome (or Edge/Firefox), install the free, open-source extension "Get cookies.txt LOCALLY" from the browser's extension store. ("Locally" matters — it reads your cookies on your own computer and doesn't send them anywhere.)
  2. Go to music.youtube.com, still signed in.
  3. Click the extension's icon → Export (Netscape format). It saves a file to your Downloads, usually named music.youtube.com_cookies.txt.
⚠️ That file is your account key. Keep it on your computer, don't email or share it. We'll delete the copy on the box automatically at the end of Step 3.
3

Send it to the box

Open Terminal (press ⌘ Space, type "Terminal", hit Return). Then run these two commands. Replace PUT_YOUR_PLAYLIST_ID_HERE with the ID from Step 1. If your cookie file has a different name, fix it in the first command.

scp ~/Downloads/music.youtube.com_cookies.txt brew@walkman-a.local:/tmp/cookies.txt

ssh -t brew@walkman-a.local '~/walkman/scripts/walkman-account.sh --cookies /tmp/cookies.txt --playlist PUT_YOUR_PLAYLIST_ID_HERE && rm -f /tmp/cookies.txt'
  • The first command copies your cookie file to the box.
  • The second tells the box to use it, set your playlist, restart, and then delete the copied cookie.
  • If it asks "Are you sure you want to continue connecting?" type yes.
  • If it asks for a password, type the box's password (ask whoever gave you the box). You won't see the characters as you type — that's normal.

When it finishes you'll see lines like ==> writing auth file, ==> playlist set, and ==> restarting Mopidy.

4

Watch it come alive

Look at the button's light over the next minute. It should go:

magenta  →  blue (starting up)  →  gently breathing green = playing!

Plug in headphones (or listen on the speaker) — your playlist is shuffling. 🎉 You're done; you never have to do this again unless the light turns magenta.

How to use it

Do this to the buttonWhat happens
Press oncePause / unpause
Press twice quicklySkip to the next song
Press & hold (~2 sec)Safe shutdown. The light turns white — once it's white it's safe to unplug. Always do this before pulling the power, so the box doesn't get corrupted.

What the light colors mean

LightMeaning
Breathing greenPlaying music
Steady amberPaused
Blinking blueStarting up, or Wi-Fi/Internet not ready (give it a minute)
Blinking magentaNeeds your YouTube login — redo Steps 2 & 3
Blinking redThe music service isn't responding — try a power-cycle (shut down first!)
Solid whiteShutting down — safe to unplug

If something goes wrong

  • "Could not resolve hostname walkman-a.local" — the box isn't on the network yet (wait a minute after plugging in), or you're on a different Wi-Fi than the box.
  • "no usable auth cookie" — the export didn't include the login. Make sure you were signed in at music.youtube.com, then redo Step 2.
  • Light goes magenta later — your cookie expired (totally normal). Just redo Steps 2 & 3 (you can leave off --playlist if your playlist hasn't changed).
  • Stuck? Power-cycle: hold the button until white, unplug, wait 5 seconds, plug back in.
