Set up your Walkman 🎵

A little music box with one button. Follow these steps once to teach it your YouTube Music, and it'll play your playlist every time you turn it on. This page is interactive — type your info into the boxes and it writes the exact commands for you. Read the "Why?" notes too; they explain what's going on.

What is this thing?

It's a tiny computer (a Raspberry Pi) wired to a speaker/headphone board and one big button with a glowing ring. Once set up, you power it on and music just plays. The button's light tells you what's happening, and the button is the only control.

First, turn it on. Plug it in (and flip its power switch, if it has one). Give it about a minute to wake up and join Wi-Fi. You should then see the light on top blinking magenta — that's the box saying: "I don't have a YouTube Music login yet — set me up!" That magenta blink is your green light to start.

Don't see a blinking magenta light after a couple of minutes? Don't guess — ask whoever gave you the box how to turn it on (and whether it's charged or needs to stay plugged in). Get the magenta blink first, then come back here.

Before you start

  • The box plugged in and on your Wi-Fi (it joins automatically; give it a minute after powering on).
  • A Mac with the Terminal app (Windows works too, but the commands differ).
  • A YouTube Music account you can sign into.
  • The "cookie helper" browser extension installed — do this now (next box); it makes Step 2 a single click.

Install the cookie helper 🧩

Never installed a browser extension before? No problem — an extension is just a little add-on for your browser, like adding an app to your phone. We need one called "Get cookies.txt LOCALLY". It's free and open-source, and "Locally" means it reads cookies on your own computer and sends them nowhere.

On Chrome (also Edge or Brave — same store):

  1. Open the extension page: Get cookies.txt LOCALLY — Chrome Web Store.
    Link not working? Search the store for it and pick the one named exactly "Get cookies.txt LOCALLY" (by kairi003).
  2. Click the blue "Add to Chrome" button.
  3. A little box pops up — click "Add extension".
  4. Pin it so you can find it later: click the puzzle-piece icon 🧩 near the top-right of your browser, then click the 📌 pin next to "Get cookies.txt LOCALLY". Now its icon sits in your toolbar — you'll click it in Step 2.

On Firefox? Search your browser's add-ons for "Get cookies.txt LOCALLY" instead.  ·  To see all your extensions any time, type chrome://extensions into the address bar and press Enter (browsers won't let a page like this one open that for you — you paste it yourself).

First, what's a "cookie"? (try it live 🍪)

Why this matters: When you log into a website, your browser saves a tiny secret note called a cookie. Each time you come back, the browser shows the cookie so the site knows it's still you — that's why you stay logged in. Our box can't open a browser, so we'll copy your YouTube Music cookie onto it so it can play music as you. The little tool below shows exactly what a cookie is.

2

Export your cookie

Do this in an Incognito window — it really matters. Your YouTube cookie is like a ticket stub that Google keeps re-printing as you browse. If you export it in your normal window and then keep using YouTube, Google re-prints the stub and the copy you gave the box stops working within an hour (this is exactly what happened on the first try!). An Incognito window gets its own ticket that doesn't get re-printed once you close it — so the box's copy keeps working for weeks.
  1. Open an Incognito window: in Chrome, menu ⋮ → New Incognito window (or ⌘ Shift N).
  2. In that window, go to music.youtube.com and sign in. Use a personal Google account if you can — school/work accounts often block this.
  3. Click the 🧩 Get cookies.txt LOCALLY extension → Export. It saves a file to your Downloads, usually music.youtube.com_cookies.txt.
  4. Close the Incognito window right away — don't browse YouTube in it again. That "freezes" the ticket so the box's copy stays good.

How to use it

Do this to the buttonWhat happens
Press oncePause / unpause
Press twice quicklySkip to the next song
Press & hold (~2 sec)Safe shutdown. Light turns white — then it's safe to unplug. Always do this before pulling power.

What the light colors mean

LightMeaning
Breathing greenPlaying music
Steady amberPaused
Blinking blueStarting up, or Wi-Fi not ready (wait a minute)
Blinking magentaNeeds your YouTube login — redo Steps 2 & 3
Blinking redMusic service not responding — power-cycle (shut down first!)
Solid whiteShutting down — safe to unplug

If something goes wrong

  • "Could not resolve hostname" — the box isn't on the network yet (wait a minute), or you're on different Wi-Fi than the box.
  • "no usable auth cookie" — make sure you were signed in at music.youtube.com, then redo Step 2.
  • Light turns magenta later — your cookie expired (normal). Reopen this page (it remembers your playlist!) and redo Steps 2 & 3.
  • Stuck? Hold the button until white, unplug, wait 5 sec, plug back in.
